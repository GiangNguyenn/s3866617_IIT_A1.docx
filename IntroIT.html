    1. Background story to IT:

      As it is shown in my education section, I am one of those non-traditional students who switch majors after realising that the current path is no longer suitable for them. As a kid growing up in a somewhat conventional Vietnamese family, its culture has been nurturing my mindset that there are specific fields/industries that females should not attempt to enter for undeniable reasons , one of them is obviously Computer Science/ Information Technology. Therefore, after highschool, my options consisted of plenty of majors, yet none of them is a IT-related one.

      Eventually, I picked Biotechnology with the hope of working as a scientist in laboratories, or as a bioinformatician who analyses Biotech-focused information. However, commencing a course only with a love for it is not sufficient by any mean. Biotechnology, or Biology in general, is an undeniably great field which has a thick history of centuries and a massive foundation with many generations contributed to it. The knowledge one can gain from it is also very practical and interesting since nature and human's body are still a mysterious treasure waiting for us to discover. However, the problem of modern biology for students is that it comprises of mostly passive learning and theoretical-based knowledge. Hence, one may have to spend years obtaining existing knowledge before he/she can stand on their own feets. I was naive enough to not be able to detect that those learning patterns does not fit my personality - which constantly seeks for innovative ideas and creative knowleadge.
